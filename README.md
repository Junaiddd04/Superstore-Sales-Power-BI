# Superstore-Sales-Power-BI

📊 Sales Store Analysis: From Raw Data to Business Insights
<br>
📌 Project Overview 
<br>
This project transforms raw retail sales data into an interactive Power BI dashboard to uncover key drivers of profitability and revenue. The analysis focuses on understanding regional performance, product category trends, and the financial impact of discounting strategies. The goal of this project is to provide actionable insights that can help a retail business optimize its pricing and inventory strategies to maximize profit margins.

🛠️ Tools & Technologies 
<br>
Microsoft Power BI: Used for data visualization, interactive dashboard creation, and overall reporting.

Power Query Editor: Utilized for data cleaning, transformation, and shaping.

DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): Implemented to calculate custom measures and key performance indicators.

📈 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
<br>
The dashboard tracks the following primary metrics at a glance:

Total Sales: $2.30M

Total Profit: $286.40K

Total Orders: 5K

Total Customers: 793

Average Order Value (AOV): $458.61

Overall Profit Margin: 12.47%

🗺️ Dashboard Features
<br>
The interactive report includes dynamic slicers for Month, Sub-Category, Segment, and Ship Mode, allowing users to filter the following visuals:

Geospatial Analysis: A map visual detailing total sales distribution across US states.

Regional Breakdown: A pie chart showing sales distribution across the West, East, Central, and South regions.

Logistics Tracking: A column chart displaying total orders processed by different shipping modes (Standard, Second Class, First Class, Same Day).

Category Performance: An area chart comparing Total Sales and Total Profit side-by-side across Technology, Furniture, and Office Supplies.

Discount Impact: A scatter/bubble chart analyzing the critical relationship between discount rates, sales volume, and resulting profit.

💡 Key Business Insights
<br>

Based on the dashboard analysis, several critical business findings were discovered:

Volume vs. Profitability (The Texas Anomaly): High sales volume does not guarantee high profitability. For example, while states like Texas generate significant sales, they yield low or negative profit margins, indicating localized operational inefficiencies or over-discounting.

The "Hero" Category: The Technology sector is the backbone of the business, leading in both total sales revenue and overall profitability.

The Cost of Discounting: There is a clear negative correlation between heavy discounting and profit. The data reveals that zero-discount products generate the highest absolute profit, suggesting that current discount strategies are eroding margins rather than driving profitable volume.

🎯 Final Conclusion & Recommendations
<br>
To improve the overall profit margin from 12.47%, the business should immediately review its discounting strategy, particularly in high-volume, low-profit states like Texas. Additionally, marketing and inventory efforts should heavily prioritize the Technology category, as it offers the best return on investment.
